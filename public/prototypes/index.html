🗡️ iSword — Slicing Prototypes

Four takes on one game: slice flying spheres for score. Same core verb, different mechanic emphasis and art direction. All mouse/touch-driven — the swipe is a stand-in for the phone-IMU swing (direction + speed), so what feels good here with a swipe is what will feel good with the real sword.

1 · Neon Arcade Slicer BASELINE

Fruit-Ninja read: orbs lobbed up with gravity, slice for combo multipliers, avoid mines. Tests the pure arcade loop — is "slash the thing" fun by itself?

2 · Blade Corridor DIRECTIONAL

Orbs fly at you down a synthwave corridor and each demands a cut in a marked direction inside a timing window. The Beat-Saber read — this maps 1:1 onto what the IMU measures best.

3 · Zen Ink Dojo TIMING

Sumi-e ink-and-paper style. A ring closes on each orb; cut exactly when it touches for a PERFECT and a red seal stamp. Timing over flailing — the iaijutsu read, and the most latency-tolerant.

4 · Sphere Storm SURVIVAL

Defend the core: the storm converges from all sides, waves escalate, kills fill a FEVER slow-mo meter. Hit-stop, screen shake, game over, restart — the full arcade juice stack.
Each file is fully self-contained (no dependencies). Open on a phone or desktop.
Pick the one that feels best with a swipe — then wire it to the IMU stream from controller.js by mapping swing direction → cut direction and angular speed → slice power.
